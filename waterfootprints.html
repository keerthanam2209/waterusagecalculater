What is a Water Footprint?

The Water Footprint represents the total volume of freshwater used to produce the goods and services we consume. It helps us understand the hidden water use behind our daily activities — from the food we eat to the clothes we wear.

Types of Water Footprints

  • Blue Water: Surface and groundwater used for irrigation, manufacturing, and domestic purposes.
  • Green Water: Rainwater stored in the soil and used by plants for growth.
  • Grey Water: The volume of water required to dilute pollutants to maintain water quality standards.

Why It Matters

Understanding our water footprint allows individuals, companies, and governments to identify areas where water can be saved and pollution minimized. It promotes transparency in global water use and supports sustainable resource management.

Examples of Water Footprints

  • 1 cup of coffee → 200 liters of water
  • 1 cotton T-shirt → 2,700 liters of water
  • 1 kg of beef → 15,000 liters of water

By making mindful choices — such as eating more plant-based foods, reusing clothes, and conserving household water — we can significantly reduce our water footprint.
